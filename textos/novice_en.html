A ‘Power Elite’ dancer may not compete in the Novice category (novice).
If a dancer performs any element above their designated level, the routine will be moved to the next level. The Novice category is designed for dancers who are just starting out..
  • A maximum of one pirouette is allowed.
  • A maximum of one fouetté is allowed.
  • A maximum of one jump is allowed without being connected to another element.
The use of cartwheels, front and back walkovers, rolls, and kip-ups is permitted, provided they are not combined with other technical elements.

Routines that are heavily loaded with advanced technical styles are not permitted.
The performance of hands-free gymnastic elements is strictly prohibited.

The Intermediate category is only available for solos, duos, and trios.
Group categories are divided only into Novice and Power Elite.

If a dancer performs any element above their level, the routine will be moved to the next level.

Permitted Elements:
  • A maximum of two pirouettes is allowed.
  • A maximum of two fouettés is allowed.
  • A maximum of two connected jumps is allowed.
  • The use of cartwheels, front and back walkovers, rolls, and kip-ups is permitted, provided they are not combined with other technical elements.

The performance of hands-free gymnastic elements is strictly prohibited.

Variations in combinations of fouettés and pirouettes.
More than three technical element are allowed.
Hands-free gymnastic elements are allowed.
